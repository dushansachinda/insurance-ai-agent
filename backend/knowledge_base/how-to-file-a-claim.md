# How to File a Claim

Filing an auto insurance claim can feel overwhelming, especially right after an accident. The process is straightforward when you know the steps, and acting quickly improves your outcome.

## Step 1: Make Sure Everyone Is Safe

Before anything else, check yourself and any passengers for injuries. If anyone is hurt, call 911. Move vehicles out of traffic if it's safe, and turn on your hazard lights. Safety always comes before paperwork.

## Step 2: Document the Scene

Use your phone to photograph:

- All vehicles involved, from multiple angles.
- License plates of every vehicle.
- The road, intersection, traffic signs, and skid marks.
- Any injuries or property damage.

Exchange names, phone numbers, driver's license numbers, license plate numbers, and insurance information with every other driver. Get contact info for witnesses if there are any.

## Step 3: File a Police Report

For accidents involving injuries, significant damage, or another driver who seems uncooperative, request a police report. Many states require one above a certain damage threshold. Get the report number — your insurer will ask for it.

## Step 4: Contact Your Insurer

Call your insurance company's claims line, use their mobile app, or visit their website to start the claim. Most insurers accept claims 24 hours a day. You'll need:

- Your policy number.
- Date, time, and location of the incident.
- Description of what happened.
- Information about other parties involved.
- Photos and the police report number.

## Step 5: Work with the Adjuster

An adjuster will be assigned to your claim. They will:

- Inspect the vehicle, either in person or via uploaded photos.
- Estimate the cost of repairs.
- Confirm what your policy covers.
- Walk you through your deductible.

Be honest and thorough. Adjusters appreciate detail and respond faster when given complete information up front.

## Step 6: Repair and Settlement

You can usually choose your own repair shop, though insurers often have preferred partners that streamline payment. After repairs, you pay your deductible and the insurer pays the rest directly to the shop. If the vehicle is totaled, the insurer pays you its actual cash value minus your deductible.

Filing promptly, documenting carefully, and communicating clearly are the three habits that turn a stressful claim into a manageable process.
