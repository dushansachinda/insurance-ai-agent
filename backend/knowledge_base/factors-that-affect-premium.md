# Factors That Affect Your Premium

Your auto insurance premium isn't a random number — it's the result of dozens of inputs that insurers use to estimate the risk of a payout. Understanding these factors helps you shop smarter and find ways to lower your bill.

## Driver-Related Factors

- **Driving record**: A clean record is the single biggest discount you can earn. At-fault accidents, speeding tickets, and DUIs raise premiums sharply, sometimes for three to five years.
- **Age and experience**: Drivers under 25 and over 70 typically pay more because data show higher claim frequency in those groups. Years of licensed driving experience also matter.
- **Credit-based insurance score**: In most states, insurers use a variation of your credit score to predict claim likelihood. Better credit usually means a lower premium.
- **Marital status**: Married drivers statistically file fewer claims, which can earn a small discount.

## Vehicle-Related Factors

- **Make and model**: Cars that are expensive to repair, frequently stolen, or have poor safety ratings cost more to insure. Sports cars and luxury vehicles are common premium drivers.
- **Vehicle age**: Newer vehicles cost more to repair or replace, raising collision and comprehensive premiums. Very old vehicles may also be costlier because of parts availability.
- **Safety features**: Anti-lock brakes, automatic emergency braking, lane-keeping assist, and anti-theft devices can earn modest discounts.
- **Annual mileage**: The more you drive, the more exposure to risk. Low-mileage commuters often qualify for a discount.

## Coverage-Related Factors

- **Coverage type**: Liability-only is the cheapest tier. Adding collision and comprehensive (or choosing "full" coverage) increases the premium meaningfully.
- **Deductible**: Choosing a higher deductible — $1,000 instead of $500, for example — lowers your premium because you're absorbing more of each claim.
- **Liability limits**: Higher liability limits (e.g., 100/300/100 instead of state-minimum) cost more but protect you better in a serious accident.

## Location-Related Factors

- **State and ZIP code**: Urban ZIP codes with high traffic, theft, or vandalism cost more than rural areas. State regulations also affect baseline rates.
- **Garaging address**: Where you park overnight affects theft risk and is part of your rate calculation.

## Discounts You Can Stack

Common discounts include multi-policy (bundling home and auto), multi-vehicle, good student, defensive driving course, paperless billing, paid-in-full, and telematics-based safe-driving programs. Asking your insurer to review every available discount once a year is one of the easiest ways to lower your premium.
