# What Is Comprehensive Coverage?

Comprehensive coverage — sometimes called "other than collision" — protects your vehicle from damage caused by events that are not collisions with another vehicle or object. Think of it as the catch-all that handles the unpredictable: weather, wildlife, theft, and acts of vandalism.

## What Comprehensive Covers

Common scenarios where comprehensive coverage pays out include:

- Theft of the vehicle or major components like a catalytic converter.
- Vandalism, such as keyed paint or smashed windows.
- Falling objects like tree limbs or debris from storms.
- Hail, flood, and other weather-related damage.
- Fire and explosion damage.
- Hitting an animal, such as a deer.
- Damage from civil disturbances or riots.

## What It Does Not Cover

Comprehensive does not pay for damage from collisions — that's what collision coverage is for. It also does not cover injuries to you or others (medical payments and bodily injury liability handle those), nor mechanical breakdowns from normal wear.

## Deductibles and Limits

Like collision, comprehensive carries a deductible. You typically have the same options: $250, $500, $1,000, or $1,500. The payout is capped at your vehicle's actual cash value. If a tree falls on your car and the repair estimate exceeds that value, the insurer will declare a total loss and pay you the value minus your deductible.

## Is It Required?

Comprehensive coverage is not required by any state. However, lenders and leasing companies almost always require it for any vehicle they finance. Once the loan is paid off, you can choose to keep or drop comprehensive based on your situation.

## Should You Buy It?

Comprehensive is usually inexpensive — often the cheapest line on a full-coverage policy — because the average claim is smaller than a collision claim. If you live in an area with severe weather, high theft rates, or a lot of wildlife, comprehensive is well worth the small monthly cost. For drivers in low-risk areas with older, low-value vehicles, dropping it can be a reasonable cost-saving choice.
